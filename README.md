Life Expectancy and Economic Indicators Analysis
Project Motivation
Life expectancy is a key indicator of a country's overall well-being.
This project explores whether economic indicators such as GDP per capita and healthcare expenditure are related to life expectancy and whether they can be used to predict it.
Business Questions
What is the distribution of life expectancy?
Is GDP per capita related to life expectancy?
Does healthcare spending influence life expectancy?
Can economic indicators help predict life expectancy?

Summary of Results
	• Life expectancy is generally concentrated between 60 and 80 years.
	• GDP per capita shows a strong positive relationship with life expectancy.
	• Healthcare expenditure also has a positive but weaker relationship.
	• A linear regression model achieved an R² score of approximately 0.38, indicating that economic indicators explain part of the variation in life expectancy.
